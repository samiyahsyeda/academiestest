Registration

We are asking for employees from local computer science businesses to help make this event a success. These people would act as experts, mentors, and judges during the event. Specific details about each postion are mentioned below.

Mentors

Mentors should be able to help students in specific categories that fits their expertise. They should have the ability to guide all skill levels present in the competition.

Shifts: 12pm-3pm & 3pm-6pm

Judges

Judges are needed for their expertise on specific fields. They will be in charge of following the rubric for each challenge or competition and awarding the team/student they believe earns the recognition.

Shift: 6pm-9pm

Experts

Experts are needed to lead the workshops we are going to be running during the Hackathon. For each workshop, there will be around 20 participants, with 4 student volunteers. Based on which cateory the workshop is (categories listed to the right), it will run for about 45 minutes to an hour.

Shift: 9am-12pm

Frequently Asked Questions...

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where participants learn, build, and share their creations over a period of time in a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. The event is 12 hours long and consists of workshops and competitions.

Who can compete?

Any middle school and high school student who has an interest in technology can compete.

Do you need to know how to code to compete?

No, you don't need any coding experience to compete! There are events for participants that have no programming experience, as well as events for people who have little to a lot of coding experience.

Is food provided?

Yes, lunch and dinner are provided.

Who can volunteer as a mentor, judge, or expert?

Employees from local computer science businesses are ideal for these positions.

Why should you join?

You can meet new people, learn new things, gain insight and new skills in technology, and have fun!
